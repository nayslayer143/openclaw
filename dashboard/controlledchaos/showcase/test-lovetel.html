the phone, not the feed

a private telephone exchange for people who'd rather hear a voice than swipe a face. there is no app to download. there is a number to call.

we never wanted to make another app.

we wanted to bring back the phone. the small ritual of picking up a receiver, waiting on a dial tone, telling someone real what you're looking for. lovetel is what's left when you take everything else away.

there is no feed. no profiles. no swipe. there is a switchboard. her name is sally. she listens — for thirty seconds or thirty minutes — and then, sometimes, the right phone rings.

three steps. no account.

  1. pick up

    dial in. tell sally — by voice — what kind of conversation you're hoping for tonight. there is no form. no profile to fill. she listens the way an old switchboard operator listened: for what you mean, not for what you check off.

  2. she listens

    sally remembers what's said, not what's typed. she keeps your vibe, not your data. anything you mark off the record never leaves the booth — not to us, not to anyone you'll be matched with.

  3. the phone rings

    if sally finds the right voice in the network, your phone rings. you decide whether to answer. you decide how long to stay on. you can hang up at any time, and nobody — including sally — gets to ask why.

what sally hears

your voice. your intent. the language you actually use. whether you laugh at your own jokes. whether you mean it when you say you're fine. the texture of what you said and the silence around it.

what we never see

names you didn't give us. faces. addresses. live recordings. anything tagged off the record. nothing here is for sale and nothing here is sold. double opt-in or no introduction — written into the schema, not just the promise.

she doesn't matchmake. she remembers.

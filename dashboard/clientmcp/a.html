The room before the room.

Foyer is the threshold between agency and client — where conversations land, brand context lives, and creative decisions are remembered.

What it is

A system of record for creative client engagements — not a project manager pretending to be one.

Inbound from five channels (Telegram, Slack, WhatsApp, SMS — and the rest in time) lands as structured artifacts. Each artifact carries its kind, its thread, its place in the project, and a quiet record of who said what when.

The brand isn't a tab in settings. It's the prompt scaffolding behind every creative decision the system helps make: every naming candidate, every mission draft, every styleguide rule. You define it once. Foyer remembers.

How it works

Two surfaces, one source of truth.

The Workspace is the agency-internal cockpit. Dense, multi-view, agent-aware. The Stage is what the client sees — curated, branded in their identity, calm. Same data, different projection. Nothing gets out of sync because nothing was ever a copy.

Six load-bearing decisions

  1. One polymorphic Artifact.

    Every nameable thing — brief, naming candidate, decision, captured message — is the same shape with a different kind. Typed storage behind a uniform wire.

  2. Clients are always free.

    Unlimited free client seats per ClientContainer. We charge agencies for seats, not their clients for access.

  3. Brand as auto-injected context.

    BrandKit ships as a cached prompt prefix into every generative call. Triage agents don't get it. Creative agents always do.

  4. Agents are typed actors.

    Name, scope, trigger, permission set, run budget, audit trail. Not a chat completion. Not a vibes-based suggestion.

  5. External agents are first-class.

    A hosted MCP server with OAuth means Claude Code, Cursor, and Codex can teammate with your Foyer workspace.

  6. Audit is the floor.

    Every write — by whom, when, through which surface — is an immutable row. 365 days standard. Seven years for regulated.

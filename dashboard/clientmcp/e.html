How it works · five movements

  1. Conversations land.

    A client sends a Telegram message, a WhatsApp voice note, a Slack thread reply. Foyer ingests it, hydrates the media, and writes a structured artifact. The webhook acknowledges in under a second; everything else happens in the worker.

  2. Triage runs.

    The Router picks which project the message belongs to. The Threader links it to the right feedback conversation. The Classifier tags intent (feedback, question, approval, scope change, decision) and urgency. All three skip BrandKit injection — they're not generating, they're routing.

  3. Brand context arrives uninvited.

    Every creative call — a naming generator, a mission drafter, a styleguide rule, an invoked skill — ships with the client's voice, palette, vocabulary, and references attached as cached prompt prefix. You define the brand once. Foyer never forgets it.

  4. Decisions are written.

    Every commitment — naming choice, mission landing, palette lock — gets recorded as a DecisionRecord artifact with title, reasoning, and timestamp. Three months from now, when the conversation re-litigates itself, the record is there.

  5. The Stage shows what's ready.

    The agency toggles client_visible on the artifacts the client should see. The Stage renders those, branded in the client's identity, behind a signed URL. The Workspace and Stage share one source of truth — the Stage is a projection, never a copy.

## Seed Content

The NFC digital business card market emerged as a distinct category between 2022 and 2025. Key dynamics:

- **Popl** raised $2.5M and became the category leader with 1M+ users by 2023, primarily targeting individual professionals and sales reps
- **HiHello** focused on enterprise B2B, landing Fortune 500 HR and sales teams with team management dashboards
- **Dot** targeted consumers and creators with a lifestyle brand aesthetic, differentiating on card design
- **Blinq** (Australian) expanded aggressively into North America with a freemium model and strong LinkedIn integration
- **Linq** rebranded to focus on events and conferences as a primary acquisition channel
- **Traditional paper business card printing** (Moo, Vistaprint) saw flat or declining volume as digital alternatives gained awareness
- **QR code fatigue** emerged as a countertrend — users reported that NFC tap felt more premium and less "pandemic-era" than QR codes
- **Salesforce and HubSpot** did not acquire any NFC card company through 2025, leaving CRM integration as a persistent market gap
- **Apple Wallet and Google Wallet** expanded passbook/wallet card support, creating a potential platform risk for standalone NFC card apps
- **Enterprise deal sizes** averaged $15–40/user/month for team plans with 10+ seats, compared to $5–10/month for individual plans
- **Event industry recovery** post-2023 drove a spike in NFC card demand at trade shows and industry conferences

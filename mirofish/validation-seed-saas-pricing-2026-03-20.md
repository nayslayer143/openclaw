# Validation Seed 2 — SaaS Pricing Compression Wave (2024–2025)

**Domain:** B2B SaaS / SMB market
**Event window:** Q3 2024 – Q1 2025 (outcomes verifiable)
**Simulation goal:** Identify winners and losers in the SMB software market as AI drives per-seat pricing toward zero

---

## Seed Content

Throughout 2024, a wave of AI-native tools entered the SMB software market at dramatically lower price points than incumbents. Key events:

- **Notion AI** bundled AI features into existing plans at no extra cost, pressuring standalone AI writing tools
- **Linear, Notion, and Coda** all cut or froze pricing as AI features became table stakes rather than premium upsells
- **Zapier and Make** faced direct competition from AI coding assistants (Claude Code, Cursor) that let non-technical users build their own automations, previously requiring a dedicated automation platform
- **HubSpot** launched a free tier with AI-generated content, squeezing boutique marketing SaaS tools
- **Intercom** raised prices for legacy tiers while launching a cheaper AI-first product, fragmenting its own customer base
- **Notion** reported that 40% of new signups in Q4 2024 came from users migrating off legacy project management tools (Asana, Monday.com)
- **Cursor and GitHub Copilot** drove a measurable decline in StackOverflow traffic as developers searched less and coded more via AI assistant
- **OpenAI** dropped GPT-4o API pricing by 50% in October 2024, triggering a race to the bottom among AI API vendors

## Questions for MiroFish

1. Which SMB SaaS categories are most vulnerable to AI-native displacement in the next 12 months?
2. What pricing strategies are defensible as AI features become free?
3. Which incumbents are most likely to successfully pivot vs. lose share?
4. What opportunities exist for new entrants in the gaps created by incumbent disruption?
